WELLINGTON: Stand-in T20I skipper Hardik Pandya on Wednesday hit back at former England captain Michael Vaughan, who criticized Team India after their semi-final exit from the T20 World Cup in Australia last week.

In a scathing column after India's loss to eventual champions England, Vaughan called the Indian team the 'biggest underperforming' unit in the history of world cricket.

Vaughan wrote that India has achieved absolutely nothing since winning the ODI World Cup at home in 2011 and has been the most under-performing white-ball team in the history.
